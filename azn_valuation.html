Key Risks

Assumptions most likely to move the thesis
1
Pipeline Execution
AZN's premium multiple assumes Dato-DXd, Tagrisso label expansions, and the Daiichi Sankyo ADC partnership deliver. Any Phase 3 failure in a flagship oncology asset would compress the growth multiple sharply and potentially re-rate the stock 15–25%.
2
Patent Cliffs
Tagrisso (osimertinib, ~$6B revenue) faces generic competition risk post-2028. Farxiga (CVRM, ~$4B+) is also patent-exposed. Combined, ~$10B+ in revenues face medium-term patent risk that the DCF only partially captures in the terminal growth fade.
3
Beta / WACC Uncertainty
The single biggest model swing: market-observed beta (0.21) → WACC ~6.0% → DCF ~$250. Sector-default (0.90) → WACC ~8.9% → DCF ~$103. The "true" cost of capital for AZN is genuinely ambiguous. The current $180 price implies an effective beta of ~0.45–0.55.
4
R&D Cost Inflation
AZN spends approximately 25% of revenue on R&D, one of the highest ratios in large-cap pharma. A sustained escalation — particularly from late-stage trial costs for biologics and ADCs — compresses FCFF faster than revenue grows, reducing the blended DCF meaningfully.
5
FX Headwinds
AZN reports in USD but earns approximately 40% of revenue in non-USD currencies (GBP, EUR, CNY). A 10% USD strengthening reduces reported revenue by ~4% and can materially suppress both reported EPS and the DCF's revenue base.
